What is this?

What it actually does

Every time you open a website, your device looks up its address — like a phone book for the internet. DNSDOH.ART does that lookup for you, but privately and cleanly. Here's what it does:

Blocks ads & trackers

Ads, trackers, and known malicious sites are stopped before they ever load — in every app on your device, not just your web browser.

Private by default

Your lookups are encrypted, so your internet provider (or the café Wi-Fi) can't see which websites you visit or sell that history.

Often faster

With less junk to download and answers served in a fraction of a millisecond, pages frequently feel snappier — not slower.

No cost, no logs

No account, no app on most devices, no cost — and nothing about your activity is recorded or stored. It runs entirely in memory.

How do I use it? Pick your device, copy one address into its DNS / "Private DNS" setting, and you're protected everywhere. The guides cover phones, laptops, and routers step by step.

Under the hood

For the curious: I built the engine too

You don't need to read any of this to use the service — but if you're technical, here's the open-source work behind it. I rewrote the core DNS software to be faster and leaner, and every change is proven with real benchmarks (not guesses) before it ships.

AdGuardHome-edge

The brain of the service. I took AdGuard Home and stripped out everything that leaks data or wastes time, then rebuilt the busy parts to barely touch memory. ~13k lines removed; zero-allocation request path.

24.6 MBleaner build · −10 MB of bloat
github.com/Ozy-666/AdGuardHome-edge-spec

dnsproxy

How requests travel in and out. I taught it to reuse connections and spread work across all CPU cores, so it stays fast even when thousands of people use it at once. Pooled connections · SO_REUSEPORT sharding.

+101%more traffic handled under load
github.com/Ozy-666/dnsproxy

dnscrypt-proxy

The part that encrypts traffic on its way to the wider internet. I slimmed it down, removed the bits that phone home, and had it independently security-audited. Static resolvers only · audited clean.

0known vulnerabilities after audit
github.com/Ozy-666/dnscrypt-proxy

urlfilter

The list-matcher that decides what's an ad or tracker. I reorganized how it searches its rules so even the trickiest ones are found instantly instead of slowing things down. AST literal indexing for regex rules.

248×faster matching under attack
github.com/Ozy-666/urlfilter

Unbound + BoringSSL

The resolver that finds each address and proves it's genuine. Checking those security signatures was eating nearly half the server's effort, so I rebuilt Unbound on Google's faster, formally-verified math library (BoringSSL) — keeping a one-command undo ready in case anything misbehaved. Statically-linked BoringSSL · full DNSSEC validation.

−26%less waiting per request · +9.6% more handled
AdGuardHome-edge-spec · §6.6 Unbound×BoringSSL
