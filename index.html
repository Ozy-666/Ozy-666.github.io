§6.6 · Cryptographic substrate

Unbound on BoringSSL

Under a 100%-signed cache-miss flood, ECDSA-P256 verification consumed ~46% of Unbound's on-CPU time. Swapping the system OpenSSL substrate for Google's BoringSSL — with formally-verified field arithmetic (fiat-crypto) and zero provider-dispatch overhead — and pinning it statically into Unbound moved the needle, with a one-command rollback kept on standby.

How I work

Engineering discipline

Verify on real CPUs

Every lock removed and every allocation eliminated is proven by pprof and benchstat — measured on production EPYC hardware, never a laptop or a synthetic rig.

Honest reporting

Rejected optimizations are documented with evidence right alongside the wins. A +3% that wasn't worth it is written down too.

Defense in depth

Impossible-by-construction invariants (a valid DNS name has no spaces) double as attack detectors and forensic tripwires.

Reversibility

Every change is feature-flagged or one-command revertible — from a pooled-connection toggle to a full OpenSSL fallback for the resolver.
